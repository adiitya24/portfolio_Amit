DfAM: Designing function into the part.

My DfAM work focuses on adding internal functionality that is difficult to create by conventional machining alone: cooling channels, lattice-based damping concepts, and custom tooling for implant-related material processing.

Thermal management

Conformal Cooling Cutting Tool

Objective: Design a cutting tool with internal conformal cooling channels to improve coolant delivery near the cutting zone.

DfAM value: Uses AM-enabled internal geometry to route channels closer to the functional region instead of relying only on straight drilled holes or external coolant.

Dynamic performance

Self-Damping Lattice Cutting Tool Concept

Objective: Develop an end mill concept with a lattice or porous region in the shank that can be filled with a high-density damping medium.

DfAM value: Uses internal architecture to create a spring–mass–damper concept where the metal body acts as the spring and the filled lattice region provides damping. This is presented as an active concept under development.

Objective: Design and print custom molds for implant-related material processing using PEEK + AMP powder.

DfAM value: Uses AM as a rapid tooling method to create custom mold cavities for compression or heat-assisted compression workflows. The printed mold acts as tooling, not the final implant.

Detailed Case Study

AISI M2 Tool Steel Across Metal AM: Process Development, Defect Mitigation, and HIP Validation

A thesis-level case study on developing AM process routes for AISI M2 high-speed tool steel across Binder Jetting, L-PBF, and DED, with the deepest work focused on Binder Jetting defect root-cause analysis, sintering/HIP densification, and performance validation.

Case Study Overview

This project summarizes my M2 high-speed tool steel work across metal additive manufacturing routes. The deepest part focused on Binder Jetting, where I developed process parameters from scratch, documented more than 25 defect types, performed root-cause analysis, and created mitigation strategies. I also evaluated sintering and HIP as post-processing routes to improve densification and understand microstructure–property changes.

Additional process-development work was performed for L-PBF and DED to compare how the same tool steel responds under different AM thermal histories, including powder–binder interaction in BJ, melt-pool behavior in L-PBF, and bead / thermal-history control in DED.

1. Binder Jetting Process Development

  • Developed Binder Jetting printing parameters for AISI M2 high-speed tool steel from scratch.
  • Studied binder saturation, binder set time, drying time, bed temperature, oscillator speed, recoater speed, roller speed, and powder behavior.
  • Created and documented more than 25 defect types during early process-development trials.
  • Performed root-cause analysis for defects related to powder flowability, binder–powder interaction, recoating, smearing, weak bonding, layer instability, cracking, shrinkage, and post-processing response.
  • Developed mitigation strategies to improve green-part quality, print consistency, and post-sintering outcomes.

2. Post-Processing: Sintering + HIP

HIP was an important post-processing step because Binder Jetting commonly leaves residual porosity after sintering. In this work, HIP was used to study pore closure, densification, hardness/property changes, and carbide/microstructure evolution. Comparing as-sintered and HIP-treated conditions helped separate the effect of printing/sintering from pressure-assisted densification.

  • Evaluated sintering temperature, time, and cooling condition effects.
  • Compared as-sintered and HIP-processed material response.
  • Connected densification and pore closure to hardness, compression response, and microstructure.

3. L-PBF and DED Process Development

  • Developed process parameters for M2 tool steel by L-PBF / SLM, focusing on process window, defect response, density, hardness, and thermal-history effects.
  • Developed DED printing parameters for M2 tool steel, focusing on energy input, bead/deposition response, porosity, density, microstructure, and hardness.
  • Compared how M2 behaves under fundamentally different AM mechanisms: binder-assisted sintering, laser powder-bed melting, and directed deposition.

4. Validation and Publications

The M2 work was validated using density, porosity, SEM/EDS, EBSD, XRD, hardness, compression testing, tribology, and image-based carbide quantification. The thesis work produced three published papers covering process development and mechanical behavior, defect remediation and carbide quantification, and wear/tribology.

Paper 1 — Process + Mechanical PropertiesBJ parameters, sintering conditions, microstructure, density, hardness, and compression behavior.
Paper 2 — Defect Remediation + CarbidesDefect identification, root-cause analysis, mitigation strategies, shrinkage, and image-based carbide quantification.
Paper 3 — Wear + TribologyEffect of sintering/cooling on friction, wear volume loss, carbide distribution, and wear mechanisms.

High-Temperature Protective Coatings for Oxidation and Decarburization Control

A manufacturing-focused case study on evaluating protective coatings for reducing oxidation and decarburization in steels during high-temperature processing.

Case Study Overview

This case study is centered on a practical industrial problem: steel components exposed to high-temperature processing can suffer oxidation, scale formation, carbon loss, and surface degradation. These effects reduce surface integrity and can compromise downstream manufacturing quality.

My work evaluated protective coatings as a manufacturing solution for reducing oxidation and decarburization. The value of this project is that it connects materials engineering directly to furnace processing, steel quality, and industrial reliability.

What I Did

  • Evaluated protective coatings for reducing high-temperature oxidation of steels.
  • Compared coated and uncoated conditions under heat-treatment / furnace-processing exposure.
  • Studied the effectiveness of coatings for reducing decarburization and preserving the near-surface region.
  • Connected the results to process reliability, surface quality, and practical manufacturing benefits.

Engineering Importance

  • Oxidation and decarburization control are important for heat treatment, forging, and steel manufacturing.
  • Effective coatings can reduce material loss, preserve surface chemistry, and improve process consistency.
  • This project demonstrates the ability to compare solutions for a real manufacturing-quality problem rather than only reporting academic data.

Detailed Case Study

Cutting Force Analysis of Straight-Flute and Helical-Flute End Mills

A manufacturing and machining case study on comparing straight-flute and helical-flute end mills through cutting-force measurements in 6061 and 7075 aluminum.

Case Study Overview

This work focuses on understanding how tool geometry influences machining-force behavior. Straight-flute and helical-flute end mills were compared to understand differences in cutting-force response, process stability, and their implications for machining performance.

This case study also connects well with my current ACE training work because it reflects CAM/CNC thinking, toolpath awareness, machining education, and the ability to explain manufacturing behavior using measured data.

What I Did

  • Compared straight-flute and helical-flute end mills in 6061 and 7075 aluminum.
  • Used a dynamometer-based setup to measure cutting-force signals during machining.
  • Analyzed force trends and connected them to tool geometry, material behavior, and machining conditions.
  • Built a process-focused explanation that is useful for tool selection, machining education, and CAM/CNC training.

Engineering Importance

  • Tool geometry influences cutting load, vibration tendency, surface finish, and process stability.
  • Force analysis improves process planning and helps explain why one tool design may behave differently from another.
  • This work shows the connection between experimental machining data and practical manufacturing decisions.
